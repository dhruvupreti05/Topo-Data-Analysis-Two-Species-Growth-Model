\subsection{Example in d=3:}

Take the start from a two-species asymmetric exclusion model on a ring with $L$ sites where each site $k$ is occupied by one of the three particle species written as $X_k$,

\[A, \quad B, \quad \Phi\]

Here $\Phi$ represents vacancy. The Markovian stochastic dynamics consists of nearest neighbor particle exchanges, $X_i X_{i+1} \to X_{i+1} X_{i}$, with the following rates,

\begin{align*}
    AB \to BA \text{ with rate $r_1$},& \quad BA \to AB \text{ with rate $r_2$} \\
    A \Phi \to \Phi A \text{ with rate $r_3$}, & \quad \Phi A \to A \Phi \text{ with rate $r_4$} \\
    B \Phi \to \Phi B \text{ with rate $r_5$}, & \quad \Phi B \to B \Phi \text{ with rate $r_6$} \\
\end{align*}

Where, 

\[r_1+r_4+r_5 = r_2+r_3+r_6,\]

to ensure equal equilibrium probabilities for all conformations of the polymer. Note this uniform distribution leads to a complete absence of stationary correlations in the thermodynamic limit $L\to\infty$. % Exalain further what this means ... ? !
